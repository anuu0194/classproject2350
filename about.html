About

Nepal, a captivating jewel in the heart of South Asia, boasts a rich tapestry of culture, geography, and spirituality. This landlocked nation is wedged between the majestic heights of the Himalayas to the north and the expansive plains of the Indian subcontinent to the south. Home to an array of ethnicities, Nepal is a melting pot of traditions, languages, and customs.

The Capital & Kathmandu

The capital city, Kathmandu, is a vibrant metropolis where ancient temples and palaces coexist with the rhythms of modern life. The country's spiritual landscape is adorned with Hindu and Buddhist shrines, including the iconic Pashupatinath Temple and the sacred Boudhanath Stupa.

Nepal's topographical diversity is awe-inspiring, ranging from the subtropical Terai region to the world's highest peaks, including the legendary Mount Everest. The Himalayas not only beckon adventurers and mountaineers but also provide a breathtaking backdrop to the daily lives of those residing in their shadow.

The Valley

The Kathmandu Valley, often referred to as the "Heart of Nepal," is a cultural and historical treasure trove nestled within the central part of the country. This valley is surrounded by the rugged hills of the Himalayas, providing a stunning natural setting for the capital city, Kathmandu, as well as the nearby cities of Bhaktapur and Lalitpur (Patan).

Kathmandu

Capital city

Kathmandu serves as the political, economic, and cultural hub of Nepal. It is a bustling metropolis where ancient architecture, vibrant markets, and modern developments coexist.

Historical and cultural Sites

Durbar Square, Swayambhunath (Monkey Temple), Pashupatinath Temple, and the Garden of Dreams are just a few of the iconic sites that showcase the rich cultural heritage of Kathmandu.

Bhaktapur

The City of Devotees

Bhaktapur, also known as Khwopa, is renowned for its well-preserved medieval architecture and a slower-paced way of life.

Durbar Square

Bhaktapur Durbar Square is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, featuring ancient palaces, temples, and courtyards that transport visitors back in time.

Patan

City of Fine Arts

Lalitpur, or Patan, is celebrated for its fine arts, particularly its metalwork, woodcarving, and traditional crafts.

Mount Everest

Sagarmatha

Mount Everest, the highest peak on Earth, is a towering icon of nature's grandeur and a symbol of human endurance.Mount Everest is part of the Himalayan mountain range and is situated on the border between Nepal and the autonomous region of Tibet in China. Its exact height is subject to change due to geological processes, but it is generally recognized as standing at 8,848 meters (29,029 feet) above sea level.
